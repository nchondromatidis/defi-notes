This page covers the necessary concepts to understand token **swap flow in Uniswap V2**.

## Concepts

### Swap

A swap is the exchange of one token for another through an automated liquidity pool in a single transaction, with no need for a traditional order book or counterparty.

You specify either an exact amount to send or an exact amount to receive, and the pool calculates the other side using its current reserves.

A 0.3% fee is deducted from each swap and distributed to liquidity providers.

### Price Impact and Slippage

Because the pool balances must move along a **multiplicative inverse curve** (a rectangular hyperbola), every trade shifts the balance of reserves and results in a new price.

This means the **effective price** paid is always slightly worse than the spot price, a phenomenon known as **price impact**.

## Methods

`UniswapV2Router2` contract (periphery) provides multiple swap methods to support different combinations of swaps.

The core distinction across all functions is two axes:
- who fixes what (exact input vs. exact output)
- and whether ETH or ERC-20 tokens are on either end

The SupportingFeeOnTransferTokens variants add a third axis:
- for tokens whose balance decreases on transfer

All of them call two helper methods:
- `_swap`: It iterates over each hop in the path array, computes the correct `amount0Out/amount1Out` for each pair, and calls `IUniswapV2Pair.swap()` on each pool, forwarding the output directly to the next pool (or to the final recipient)
- `_swapSupportingFeeOnTransferTokens`: A variant of _swap for tokens that take a fee on every transfer (e.g., deflationary tokens). Instead of relying on pre-calculated amounts, it reads the pair's actual token balance after transfer to determine `amountInput`, then computes `amountOutput` on-chain, since the received amount may differ from what was sent.

All router swap methods call `UniswapV2Pair.swap()` method, which will be the focus of this document.
